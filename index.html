Where the data comes from

Every piece of the console is grounded in a public or org-accessible source. No black boxes.

NB court dockets

Origin: www3.gnb.ca/cour/WebDockets — the public PDF docket feed the Court of NB publishes.

Mirrored in: freefred/index/dockets.jsonl

Every docket row on this console links back to the source PDF.

CanLII NB decisions

Origin: canlii.org/en/nb — Canadian Legal Information Institute.

Mirrored in: freefred/cases/nb/ and freefred/index/canlii_nb.jsonl

Docket ↔ CanLII joins

How they're linked: surname match, charge section match, docket-number exact match, decision-within-30-days-of-hearing. Each match records its signals and a confidence score.

File: freefred/index/joins.jsonl

Canon (doctrine)

What it is: the rulebook the tool follows — scope, hard filters, role classification, records-recall doctrine, professional-registry doctrine, pattern-access doctrine.

Folder: freefred/canon/

CanFree (org-private)

What it is: the national corpus and the broader case-law substrate. Private to EVEglyphDesign org members.

Repo: github.com/EVEglyphDesign/canfree (org access required)
